Slide 7 · The Risks

Risks & kill signal.

The biggest risk isn't that construction is too hard. It's that we invest vertical resources when horizontal would've worked.
"They don't adopt tech"
Card deployment ≠ ERP deployment. No implementation, just swipe. Buyer is the CFO, not the field worker.
"ERP integrations = years"
Don't build until data says which ERPs. Most are on QuickBooks — already integrated.
"Flex tried and left"
$225M raised, then pivoted horizontal. Understand why before we commit.
"Acquisition risk"
Divvy lesson: "No clear idea who their customers were." Siteline = AR (subs). Ramp = AP (GCs). Different buyer.
Closer to 2027 AI CFO
Construction = new spending data (materials, equipment, subs). More data categories → smarter agents → wider moat.
Further from 2027 AI CFO
20 PMs, 1 per surface. A construction PM is a PM NOT building AI CFO for existing 45K customers.
Kill criteria
If construction customers behave like any other Ramp customer — same features, same usage patterns, no vertical-specific adoption — then there's no vertical. And that's a valid answer.
