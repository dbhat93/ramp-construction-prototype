Slide 3b · The Pain

Construction finance pain points.

What Michael and Oscar deal with every day — and where Ramp can help.
📊

Job Costing

Every dollar tracked to project, cost code, phase. CSI MasterFormat (50 divisions, 100s of codes). 10-50 active jobs simultaneously. Field purchases never get coded.

74-day avg cycle
💰

Cash Flow

"Profitable on paper, broke in the bank." Costs front-loaded, revenue on 60-90 day cycles. Retainage (5-10%) locked 12-24 months. Growth makes it worse — more backlog = more cash out.

43% lack reserves
🧾

Expense Tracking

Paper receipts in truck consoles. Personal cards with reimbursement at month-end (maybe). No project coding on field purchases. Construction One: 75 spreadsheets consolidated manually.

40→10 hrs with Ramp
📄

AP/AR Complexity

200-500+ invoices/month. AIA G702/G703 progress billing. Lien waivers across 50+ subs (miss one = property exposed to lien). Retainage tracking per sub. Change orders cascade.

50+ subs/project
🔒

Fraud & Theft

Ghost employees (most prevalent in construction, 18-month detection). Material theft from job sites. Fuel siphoning. Invoice fraud. Kickbacks. 23% of occupational fraud = payroll schemes.

5% revenue lost
⚖️

Compliance

Davis-Bacon (federal projects >$2K). Certified payroll weekly (WH-347). Insurance certs for every sub — expire and must renew. Sales tax exemptions vary wildly by state.

70% no tech plan
The underbidding trap
Subs underbid because they don't know their true overhead per job. They price on gut feel and historical averages, then get burned when a job runs 15% over. Ramp could show: "Your last 10 HVAC installs averaged $X in overhead. This bid covers $Y. You're underpriced by 12%."

Slide 8 · The Risks

Risks, metrics & kill signal.

The biggest risk isn't that construction is too hard. It's that we invest vertical resources when horizontal would've worked.
"They don't adopt tech"
Card deployment ≠ ERP deployment. No implementation, just swipe. Buyer is the CFO, not the field worker.
"ERP integrations = years"
Don't build until data says which ERPs matter. Most are on QuickBooks — already integrated.
"Flex tried and left"
$225M raised, pivoted horizontal. Understand why before we commit. Could be credit risk, unit economics, or TAM.
"Acquisition risk"
Divvy lesson: "No clear idea who their customers were." Siteline = AR (subs). Ramp = AP (GCs). Different buyer.

Leading indicators

  • Field worker receipt submission rate (SMS)
  • CSI code adoption vs generic job-cost mapping
  • Project code accuracy (AI auto-coded vs manual override)
  • Time: card swipe → coded transaction (days → minutes)
  • Multilingual engagement rate vs English-only

Core business metrics

  • Card volume from construction (total + per-customer)
  • Construction customers: new vs converted from horizontal
  • Avg spend per construction customer vs Ramp overall
  • Revenue per customer (interchange + SaaS)
  • Net revenue retention in construction segment
Kill criteria
If construction customers behave like any other Ramp customer - same features, same usage patterns, no vertical-specific adoption - then there's no vertical. We learned cheaply and can quickly redirect.
